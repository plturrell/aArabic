## Executive Summary

This DOI documents the accounts payable process for vendor invoice payments, Cross Border Recharges (CBR), and manual payments for the Domestic Business Unit (DBU) in Bahrain. The process ensures compliance with Group Business Expenditure Standards and provides proper controls and audit trails.

### Key Risks
- Fraud and bribery risk
- Duplicate payments
- Compliance with Group Delegated Authorities

---

## 1. Process Overview

### 1.1 Mandatory Systems
As per Group Business Expenditure Standard, the following systems are mandatory:
- **eProcurement** - For purchase orders and standard invoice processing
- **International Mobility (IM) / HR** - For employee-related payments
- **SODA** - For travel and expense management

### 1.2 Payment Types
1. **PO Invoices** - Standard invoices with Purchase Orders
2. **Manual Invoices** - Non-PO invoices (exceptional basis only)
3. **Manual Payments** - In-country payments (one-off or recurring)
4. **Cross Border Recharges (CBR)** - Managed centrally by GBS

---

## 2. Detailed Procedures

### 2.1 PO Invoice Processing

**Process Flow:**
1. Vendor scans invoice with PO number to AP team
2. Invoice interfaces into Tax-Q system
3. Finance team processes in country finance queue

**Required Actions:**
- Provide Purpose of Payment (POP) - Refer to Annexure 2
- Classify invoice into VAT categories (see Section 2.2)

**Responsibility:** Country Finance / Payment Requester

---

### 2.2 VAT Classification Categories

#### 2.2.1 Full Tax Invoice
**Requirements:**
- Label "Tax Invoice"
- Supplier name, address, and Tax Registration Number (TRN)
- Customer name and address (SCB Bahrain)
- Date and sequential tax invoice number
- Description, quantity, value, unit price, date of supply
- Discounts (if any) and net value
- VAT rate and VAT amount
- Total amount inclusive of VAT
- Exchange rate (for foreign currency invoices)
- Exemption statement (if applicable)
- Profit margin scheme reference (if applicable)

#### 2.2.2 Simplified Tax Invoice
**Acceptable when:** Consideration does not exceed BHD 500 (inclusive of VAT)

**Missing elements (acceptable):**
- Label "Tax Invoice"
- Customer name and address
- Sequential invoice number
- Quantity, unit price details
- Discount details
- Exchange rate
- Exemption statement

#### 2.2.3 Unregistered Vendor Purchases
Invoices from vendors/government authorities not registered with NBR

#### 2.2.4 Zero Rated Purchases
**Includes:**
- Domestic and International transportation
- Basic food items
- Healthcare services, Medicines and medical equipment
- Educational services
- Construction services for new buildings
- Domestic oil and gas

**Note:** Must meet tax invoice requirements despite zero rating

#### 2.2.5 Exempt Purchases
**Includes:**
- Real estate supplies
- Financial services

#### 2.2.6 Tax Credit Note
Credit notes issued by taxable person with VAT amount

#### 2.2.7 Reverse Charge Invoice
**Process:**
- Invoice from overseas supplier
- VAT amount calculated and written on invoice by Country Finance
- VAT calculated in FCY and LCY using central bank exchange rate as of invoice date

#### 2.2.8 GCC Tax Invoice
For suppliers based in GCC states (Currently not in use)

#### 2.2.9 Inter Business Unit Transfers
For interbranch transactions (CBRs)

#### 2.2.10 Out of Scope
**Includes:**
- Supplies provided before 1st January 2019
- Multi-purpose vouchers (MPV) - per NBR Section 4.6

#### 2.2.11 Other
Currently only Electricity and Water Authority (EWA) bills - registered but VAT not charged yet

### 2.3 Manual Invoice Processing (Non e-Proc Invoices)

**Definition:** Invoices that do not go through the PO route

**Required Documents:**
1. Invoice
2. Non e-proc memo approved by:
   - Cost centre owner
   - Country supply chain manager
   - CFO or delegate
   - Refer to Annexure 1

3. Scan memo and invoice to: **BH, APUeInvoice**

**Control Checks:**
- ✅ All approvals obtained (cost centre owner, supply chain manager, CFO/delegate)
- ✅ Payment made to vendor as per invoice (unless clear vendor instruction states otherwise)
- ✅ Invoice amount within approver DOA limit per GDAM
  - **Exception:** GSAM legal and professional fees (confirmed by Group Corporate Secretariat)

**Responsibility:** Country Finance / Payment Requester

---

### 2.4 Manual Payments (In-Country Payments)

**Definition:** Payments not made via standard invoice payment process or PeopleSoft AP

**Classification:**
- One-off Manual Payments
- Recurring Manual Payments

#### 2.4.1 One-Off Manual Payments

**Required Documents/Approvals:**
1. Invoice
2. Non e-proc memo (approved by cost centre owner, supply chain manager, CFO/delegate)
3. Statement of nature of expense (evidence it's one-off, not recurring)
4. Supplier number in PSAP vendor (if applicable) OR Screening confirmation
   - **Note:** Screening results valid for 48 hours
5. Managing Director (MD) approval (or CEO if no MD in country)
6. CFO approval
7. Approval document indicating account to be debited
8. OTT form for telex transfer (Annexure 3)
9. Submit to Operations to print cheque

#### 2.4.2 Recurring Manual Payments

**Requirements:**
- All requirements from one-off payments EXCEPT MD/CEO approval
- **MANDATORY:** Written dispensation approved by policy owner must be held by originating department

#### 2.4.3 Post-Payment Actions

**After any manual payment:**
1. Record payment in eOPS: https://eopsvip05.hk.standardchartered.com:9443/eopssec/index.jsp
   - **Note:** Quarterly CFO log retrieval and approval required
   - Upload CFO approval in eOPS for quarterly manual payments CST check
2. Mark invoice as PAID
3. Send to APU team (GBS Bangalore/Chennai) for:
   - Recording in PeopleSoft Accounts Payable
   - Posting GL entry
   - Use AP standard memo (Annexure 4)

**Payment Suspense Accounts:**
| BU | CCY | eBBS Account No |
|---|---|---|
| DBU | BHD | 09-0906743-50 |
| DBU | USD | 09-0906743-50 |

**Note:** All manual payments paid through payment suspense account

#### 2.4.4 Control Checks for Manual Payments
- ✅ All approvals obtained (cost centre owner, CFO/delegate)
- ✅ Dispensation in place for recurring payments
- ✅ Vendor onboarded OR screening performed
- ✅ Payment executed within 48 hours of screening result
- ✅ Payment made to vendor as per invoice (unless clear vendor instruction)
- ✅ Invoice amount within approver limit per GDAM
  - **Exceptions:** GSAM legal/professional fees, Tax/VAT payments (confirmed by Group Corporate Secretariat)

**Responsibility:** Country Finance / Payment Requester

### 2.6 Clearing Payment Suspense Account

**Process:**
1. Extract PSGL account **140-288843-800** for required period
2. Reconcile to trial balance account (YTD balance of 288843)
3. Extract transaction archival from eBBS for payment suspense account:
   - BHD: 09-0906743-50
   - USD: 09-0906743-50
4. Compare amounts in both extracts
5. If amounts match, post PSGL entry to move balances from payment suspense to respective cost centres and GL accounts

**Clearing Requirements:**
- All items should be cleared in the same month
- Aged items must be escalated per escalation grid

**Aging Calculation:**
```
Aging = Current Date - Value Date - Life Span
```

**Control Checks:**
- ✅ GL extract reconciled to eBBS extract
- ✅ Payments properly approved
- ✅ Correct PSGL chart fields to clear balances

**Responsibility:** Country Finance

**Approval Matrix:**
- Preparer: Finance Manager
- Covered for preparer: Finance Manager
- Approver: CFO
- Covered for approver: Finance Senior Manager

## 12. Recommendations and Best Practices

### 12.1 Process Improvements
1. **Automation:** Consider automating VAT classification where possible
2. **Screening:** Ensure vendor screening is completed well in advance to avoid 48-hour rush
3. **Documentation:** Maintain centralized repository for all approvals and documents
4. **Reconciliation:** Perform monthly reconciliation of payment suspense accounts

### 12.2 Risk Mitigation
1. **Fraud Prevention:**
   - Verify all approvals before processing
   - Cross-check vendor details
   - Validate invoice authenticity

2. **Duplicate Payment Prevention:**
   - Check invoice numbers against processed invoices
   - Verify PO numbers are not duplicated
   - Review payment suspense account regularly

3. **Compliance:**
   - Regular training on VAT classification
   - Stay updated with NBR guidelines
   - Monitor changes in Group Business Expenditure Standards

### 12.3 Training Requirements
- VAT classification training (all categories)
- Group Business Expenditure Standards
- DOA limits and exceptions
- System training (eProcurement, Tax-Q, eOPS)

## 13. Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist for each payment:

- [ ] Payment type identified (PO Invoice / Manual Invoice / Manual Payment / CBR)
- [ ] All required approvals obtained
- [ ] VAT classification completed correctly
- [ ] Purpose of Payment (POP) provided
- [ ] Vendor verified (onboarded or screened)
- [ ] Amount within DOA limits (or exception documented)
- [ ] All documents attached and scanned
- [ ] Payment suspense account cleared (if applicable)
- [ ] Payment recorded in eOPS (for manual payments)
- [ ] Invoice marked as PAID and sent to APU team
- [ ] Documentation filed for audit
